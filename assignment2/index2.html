Mental Health Awareness Amoung College Students of Color

Research on Mental Health in College Students

Mental Health amoungst college students remains a serious issue that continues to be studied. However, students of color face many challenges, with higher rates of mental health concers, such as anxiety or depression. Despite these statistics, research addressing unique experiences and mental health needs of students of color is still limited.

Mental Health Statistics:

  • College students of color (POC) often experience higher rates of mental health issues compared to their white peers, including higher levels of depression and anxiety,
  • Compared to 61% of white students, 75% of Black college students report they tend to keep their feelings about how hard college is to themselves.
  • Students of color may experience increased emotional distress during their first year of college due to potential cultural adjustment difficulties.
  • Experiencing microaggressions on campus can contribute to increased stress and mental health concerns for students of color.
