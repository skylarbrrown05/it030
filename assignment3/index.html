The Normalization of Fast Fashion

Fast Fashion is a problem mainly amoung Gen Z, who are both eco-consious and drawn to affordable trends. Brands like Shein and Temu offer low-priced clothing that appeal to younger consumers seeking to stay fashionable without overspending. As a current college student, I see firsthand the amount of people who normalize these websites.

Although awareness of fast fashions negative impact is growing, studies show that when young people learn about the environmental harm caused by brands like Shein, they are less likely to buy from them. With more people bringing these issues to light, we can reduce the harm on not only the working conditions, but the environment as well.
